What is GMDH?

GMDH (Group Method of Data Handling) is a type of self-organizing modeling algorithm, developed by Alexey Ivakhnenko in the 1960s. It's an advanced machine learning method designed for building predictive models by automatically selecting the best model structure from data. It builds layers of polynomial models, evolving the structure layer-by-layer like a neural network, but with automatic model selection at each stage.

Why Use GMDH?

Unlike traditional models, GMDH can discover the best mathematical relationships hidden in the data without prior assumptions. It can reduce overfitting through its evolutionary layer-by-layer approach and is particularly powerful for time series forecasting, system modeling, and predictive analytics. Its simplicity and adaptability make it perfect for complex real-world systems like weather prediction.

Application in Wind Prediction

In wind speed forecasting, GMDH helps by modeling complex non-linear relationships between temperature, humidity, and other atmospheric variables.
